Pythagorean Theorem

The Pythagorean Theorem is one of the most well-known formulas in math. It states that for any right triangle, the sum of the squares of sides A and B equals the square of the hypotenuse (side C). The theorem is named after Pythagoras, though it was known to many cultures before his time. The theorem has been proven using various methods.
